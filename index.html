Has Alibaba changed SCMP?

There is a maritime sign in the nautical international signal flag, indicating that “My ship is willing to communicate with your ship”.

The inspiration from the signal was applied into a logo by the South China Morning Post (SCMP) in February 2018.

SCMP is one of Hong Kong's most credible and competitive English-language media, with a history of 113 years.

And it tried to use the logo to express that "I want to talk with you, I want to communicate with you.”

However, in November 2015, Alibaba announced that it would buy SCMP Alibaba and in April 2016 became the Post’s parent company. Many people speculating that this acquisition may affect the objectivity and attitude of the Post.

Does media ownership really affect editorial independence? Is SCMP really changing?

"We believe that through mergers or acquisitions, the largest shareholder of the new owner can obtain the right to speak, determine the business practices, resource allocation and personnel appointments of the media organizations, thus affecting the number of people in the news report, the subject matter choice, the content etc. Therefore, the mergers and acquisitions and investment actions of Beijing represent not only the expansion of their discourse, but also their ability to control the media. - Du Yaoming, Xie Tao, Qu Jia Ling 2018 HKJA Annual Report 2018

我们相信，通过并购或入股，新东主最大股东可取得话事权，决定传媒机构的经营手法、资源分配和人事任命，从而影响新闻报道的人手多少、题材取舍、内容轻重、角度焦点等等，因此北京及同路人的并购及投资举动，既代表其言论版图的扩张，也标示其控制媒体能力的提升。——杜耀明、谢颂昕、区家鳞 2018香港言论自由年度报告

With the recognition that ownership is important, there is a second sentence: ownership is just one of many factors that influence news content. Because the operation of a professional media is a complex system, the main influencing factors include: position in market, readers’needs, and very important: the independence of editors and the professionalism they hold. In a media company, the editorial reporter is of course working for the big boss, but the profession they are engaged in is not a screw. (the example that SCMP was bought by Jack Ma remains to be observe, but at least at present it hasn’t brought any obvious changes to the content.) - Fang Kecheng, "NewsLab"

所有制很重要，但在承认所有制很重要的前提下，还有第二句话，那就是：所有制只是影响新闻内容的诸多因素之一，在成熟、专业的新闻媒体，有很多其他因素会削弱所有制带来的影响。因为一家专业媒体的运作是一个复杂的系统，其中主要的影响因素包括：市场定位、读者需求，以及非常非常重要的：编辑记者的主体性，以及他们所秉持的新闻专业主义。在一家媒体公司里，编辑记者当然是在给大老板打工，但他们所从事的职业，并不是言听计从的螺丝钉。（马云的例子还有待观察，但至少现在的《南华早报》和马云买下来之前没有太大区别）——方可成

We interviewed different related people, such as journalists working on SCMP and some professors. They held different views and the former chief editor of SCMP said he gave no comment but the judgement can depend on the observation after analyzing the content of the SCMP during these years.

About the dataset

One of the most difficult part is to get the dataset at the beginning. We called and consulted with the SCMP and they told us even member can only read the news during the latest two months, and employees of the Post also can't read all the news for a few years.

Close to giving up this topic…luckily, we finally found a database called ProQuest from the website of Hong Kong Baptist University (HKBU). There are news texts from the Post from 1993 to 2018. Some data are missing, such as bylines in news in recent years. In total, we scraped headlines of the SCMP articles from 2010 to 2018 and obtained 263,107 data points.

At the same time, we also obtained a questionnaire made by a group of master students from HKBU. The samples, 80 students said that the main reason for them to read the Post was that they think it had a high degree of credibility.

Robert Kuok and Jack Ma, more news about Jack Ma

Before analyzing the data, we had some directions and clues through interviews or other conversations.

Gary Liu, CEO of SCMP, gave a speech in HKBU in October. Unsurprisingly he was asked that if the editorial board is still choosing its own topics? Gary said at least at present the Alibaba doesn’t give any pressure to their editors and he hope it will not happen in the future.

Some held the view that the change may took place as early as in 1997, the handover year when the tycoon Robert Kwuk owned the SCMP.

Robert Kwuk is the Malaysia’s richest man, founding the Shangri-La. Robert’s family has close ties with Chinese senior government officials due to the economic and social contribution to the mainland.

Some observers pointed out that Robert helped purifying the criticism of the Chinese government, but Robert denied. According to Huxiu media, on June 7, 2012, a foreign part-time editor of the Post, Alex Price, wrote an email to the editor-in-chief Wang Xiangwei, who believed that SCMP had lost credibility in several news.

Guo Zhongshi, a professor at the Department of Communication at HKBU, believes that after bought by Alibaba, editors in the Post may think of their boss are Jack Ma while writhing, and then they are prone to cover some of what Jack Ma loves to hear.

It is difficult to define which content is Jack Ma loves to listen to, but a few days ago, the Post had a very interesting headline: Jack Ma is a Communist Party member, so what?

A source said that Jack Ma’s Alibaba is a company which has the closet relationship with the government and many executives in Alibaba was worked for government departments. By scraping the headlines from 2010 to 2018, we found that the number of times mentioning Jack Ma was increasing, and that the number of his company Alibaba also appeared more than that of other companies in the BAT category.

Considering main audience of the Post, they are not mainly readers in China,but global readers.Alibaba’s vice president Joe Tsai also indicated that the purpose of buying SCMP is to show a better image of China. All kinds of signs have increased speculation that the Post has become pro-Beijing.

What’s the problem about G20?

Zhang Jieping, former editor chief of Initium media, provided a clue to news analysis, which is to find some news on specific days such as G20. Zhang thinks the Post is still reporting some sensitive issues. In fact, we can still see about Liu Xiaobo, June 4th accident. But at the same time, positive and propaganda reports are increasing,showing the good image of China to the world.

Zhang said the news coverage of G20 summit of the Post become highly similar to of newspapers such as China Daily and Global Times With this as a clue, we specially analyzed the news appeared on G20 from SCMP and also the news from China Daily. In 2015, the Post and Alibaba are still in the takeover transition phase and there was almost no word about G20 appearing in the headlines on November 15 and 16.

By contrast, China Daily had a series of news reports related to G20 in 2014.

And then in 2016, the 11th G20 summit was held in China, to be precise, the city or Hangzhou which has Alibaba. This year the Post started to report news about G20 but far less than in 2017.

Now, the South China Morning Post has a G20 column.

What can we talk about Xi?

we analyzed annual word frequency of news of during 2010-2018, and we pay much attention to the 2012 and 2018, The two landmark years for China’s leaders. You need to specify the width and height of the iframe, making it non-responsive to different screens.

As you can see, in the 2012, Bo Xilai and Xi Jinping appears many times in headlines of news and this year China was preparing to replace its leaders.

By 2018, the present Xi amended the constitution and removed the president’s term limit and emphasized the party leads the government, military, society..

It was also a huge change, however, the name of Xi didn’t appear as many times as it did in 2012. The assumption is that Was the Post deliberately evading something?

However, the Post has been the first to report that the Communist Party's military heavyweight tigers who was being investigated, and we can still see there are news talking about Xi's amendment.

At the same time, based on limited data, we roughly found that the number of Post's foreign journalists shows a upward trend, and all of these may exactly be a signal that SCMP proves its neutrality and objectivity to the outside world.

It seems that change is happening in an unobvious way and is hard to perceive.
